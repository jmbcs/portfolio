About Me

My Journey

My journey into software engineering began during my studies at the University of Aveiro. While working on my thesis—an image retrieval system that associated images with text—I discovered the power and flexibility of Python. That project turned a passing curiosity into a lasting passion for programming.

Since then, I’ve worked in both hybrid and remote environments, building backend systems, microservices, and scalable APIs using technologies like Django, FastAPI, Golang, and PostgreSQL. From contributing to large-scale property management platforms at GuestReady to developing monitoring systems and PoCs for HPC environments at Wavecom, every experience has shaped the developer I am today.

What really drives me is the thrill of problem-solving—it gives me a certain high to face a challenge I don’t yet know how to solve, and then piece things together until the solution clicks.

It’s this constant learning and creative process that keeps me excited about writing code.

Goals & Aspirations

    Professionally, my goal is to grow into a technical leadership role where I can guide and support a small team working on a product. I see myself mentoring junior and mid-level developers, helping them grow while fostering a collaborative and healthy team dynamic.

    At the same time, I want to stay close to the code. Writing and reviewing code is something I genuinely enjoy, and I believe being hands-on helps me make better architectural and strategic decisions. Ideally, I want to strike a balance between technical leadership and active development—building meaningful software while enabling others to do the same.
